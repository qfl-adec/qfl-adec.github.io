Quantum Federated Learning for Anomaly Detection in Electrical Consumption

Advancing Energy Monitoring with Quantum Federated Learning for Secure and Efficient Anomaly Detection.

About the Project

1. Project Overview

  • Development of a Quantum Federated Learning (QFL) infrastructure for training AI models.
  • Utilizes electricity consumption data from multiple energy companies.
  • Aims to enhance Anomaly Detection for:
    • Identifying unauthorized operations
    • Detecting measurement defects
    • Mitigating energy losses
    • Optimizing energy distribution and blackout management

2. Research & Technological Advancements

  • Focus on Quantum Machine Learning (QML) techniques for:
    • Advanced data analysis
    • Proactive anomaly detection
  • Federated learning ensures data privacy:
    • Prevents direct sharing of sensitive energy consumption data
    • Allows organizations to train highly customized AI models

3. Operational Implementation

  • Development of a dedicated dashboard for real-time monitoring.
  • Compliant with AI Act regulations to ensure ethical AI deployment.
  • Integrates a Human-In-The-Loop (HITL) approach:
    • Operators actively monitor and manage the learning process
    • Ensures AI decision-making remains supervised and transparent
  • All operations securely recorded on a blockchain for accountability and traceability.
